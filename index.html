03 Case Studies

Building the First Large-Scale Crowdsourced Dataset for Portuguese Sign Language

01
M.Sc. Thesis (in progress) · Interactive Technologies Institute × Universidade Católica Portuguesa · Feb 2026 – Present

ProblemIsolated Sign Language Recognition (ISLR) research for Portuguese Sign Language was blocked by the absence of a large-scale dataset — lab recording and web scraping don't scale or don't capture natural signing.

My roleResearch for the dataset and its integration into a live public LGP dictionary platform, from data collection design through to a shipped feature.

What I did

  • Designed crowdsourcing as the only viable path to scale, after ruling out lab recording and scraping, and built a crowdsourcing tool to collect the dataset
  • Met with APS (Associação Portuguesa de Surdos) to bring the Deaf community's insights into how the work was defined and built
  • Set the core research framing with my advisor: collect video with consent, anonymize as post-processing, and compare ISLR performance on anonymized vs. non-anonymized data
  • Shipped a new contributor module on the live dictionary platform (SvelteKit + Supabase, deployed on Cloudflare), including a new user role and admin-controlled feature visibility

Where it standsPassed the thesis proposal defence with 18/20; currently finishing the remaining work and writing up.
